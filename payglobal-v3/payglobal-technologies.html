HOW PAYGLOBAL DEBIT SYSTEM WORKS:

Direct debits are a popular way to pay and our debit system allows you to collect one-off, regular or variable payments, using either our online tool or API. Your customers also benefit from the peace of mind knowing that they are protected by the Direct Debit Guarantee.

  • There’s no need to worry about long delays in processing funds, as we automatically pay out to your bank account three working days after the money has been taken from your customer.
  • It’s easy for your customer, too- they will receive an email with a link to our payment authorisation page. All they have to do in order to authorise the direct debit is click on the link and complete the secure checkout.
  • Once your customer has authorised your direct debit, you can automatically collect payment from them by adding them to a payment plan. The customer will receive a notification from us every time payment is collected.
  • You can submit an authorisation from PayGlobal by adding a customer to a payment plan or sending to them via an email link. Once the customer has clicked the link, they will be taken to a secure online payment page which will allow them to set up authorisation for future payments.
  • You can easily customise when customers’ payments arrive by selecting the frequency and duration of payment, as well as the day and date payment is submitted.
  • As long as payment hasn’t been submitted, you also have the option to cancel payment, remove a customer from one payment plan or add them to a new one.
  • All of these features can be done quickly and easily from your dashboard, which provides you with information in real-time, meaning that you can accurately track the status of payments, transactions and customers whenever you need to.

PayGlobal debit system is compatible with smart devices, including phones and tablets. Our payment pages are compatible with all Internet browsers.


You can accept payments in the following European countries:

Austria, Belgium, Cyprus, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, the Netherlands, Portugal, San Marino, Slovakia, Slovenia and Spain. Your business can be registered anywhere in the world, but you must have a Euro account registered in either the UK or a Eurozone country. We support payments in sterling (GBP) and EUR.

As long as you have a UK/EU bank account, it’s quick and simple to get started- and you could be taking your first payments within minutes! Everything is automated and PayGlobal does all the hard work for you, by collecting and tracking all of your payments.

Using PayGlobal’s direct debit system means that you can assure your customers that their security is paramount. Our security systems are military-grade and all bank details are encrypted using RSA encryption; we communicate with banks using secure channels only.

Our system also works for you. Because our system is built on the Direct BACS system, you won’t lose customers when their cards are cancelled or expired, and the system has a high rate of customer retention. We use modern, RESTful API and it’s quick and simple to add PayGlobal to your site or app- you could be up and running in minutes. (We even offer API libraries to help you!)

Payglobal Gateway

Payment gateways are the software and servers that allow communication between the customer’s bank and the merchant’s bank, and that allows transactions to proceed.

With cybercrime on the rise and becoming increasingly sophisticated, security is an essential part of all payment gateways, as data such as credit card numbers and other identity information needs to be protected from criminals. Anyone who has access to card information must abide by the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS or PCI).

Most online orders are completed using the safe HTTPS protocol, which means that the information provided is communicated to the merchant via secure means. PayGlobal’s payment gateway service is a PCI Level 1 certified solution. This means that it provides merchants with secure and encrypted real-time credit card payment authorisation for card-not-present transactions. Let us help you offer a safe, secure service- both for you and for the peace of mind of your customers.

PayGlobal exclusive merchants can also benefit from a range of other features, including

Processing Volume Distribution and Routing (Load Balancing): Supports multiple merchant accounts simultaneously in order to allow you to distribute your transaction volume.

Velocity Control: Protect yourself from fraudulent activity- our extensive filters detect and block any suspicious transactions.

Virtual Terminal: Internet access allows our merchants to track and access their accounts anywhere in the world, allowing you to manually authorise and process all of your transactions.

Transaction Filters: By pre-defining your rules of acceptance associated with factors such as Address Verification, Card Certification Value and IP address, you can easily protect yourself from high risk customers and fraudulent transactions.

The PayGlobal Gateway allows you to easily refund, repeat or cancel transactions, download data for your accounts and reconcile receipts. Transactions are quickly authorised and payment will reach you in a matter of days, saving you time that can be spent elsewhere on your business.

PayGlobal also offers merchants extra options whenever a cardholder purchases a service or product. As well as allowing real-time transactions to take place, PayGlobal allows merchants to convert currencies between countries, as well as bridging both language and payment methods. It is usual for payment gateways to charge a fee to those who use these services.

Payglobal Chargeback Protection

Although important for consumer protection, chargebacks can be frustrating for merchants when some consumers choose to abuse them. Therefore, it’s essential for every online merchant to have a secure service in place that’s designed to both detect possible security threats and to also allow any necessary resolutions to be as straightforward as possible.

What do online merchants need to know about chargebacks? How can a secure chargeback solution be beneficial?

Cardholders can dispute a card transaction up to six months after making a purchase. This includes both debit and credit cards. This is called a chargeback and often occurs when a cardholder does not recognise or disagrees with a card purchase. By knowing how to prevent chargebacks, you can save both time and money. If not handled correctly, you may find your business held liable for the financial implications of chargebacks.

PayGlobal offers an advanced chargeback solution service, which includes features that help you to detect fraudulent credit cards and possible chargebacks. Our services flag up any potential issues and help you to deal with them as quickly as possible, before they become a real problem. By implementing detection features alongside your payment system means that you are less likely to receive chargebacks and the problems they can cause.

A chargeback service is vital if you trade online, nationally or globally. You need to have payment processing software that detects these issues accurately, so that they can be dealt with quickly- meaning that the rest of your transactions can run efficiently. As this is one of the more complex aspects of online sales, it helps to work with a reliable partner with knowledge and experience in this area.

Could I be responsible for chargebacks?

There are some instances in which a merchant might be held liable for all chargebacks incurred. These are:

If the transaction was processed in any way that could be confusing or unclear to the consumer

If the merchant did not honour their terms of sale.

If the merchant did not follow the guidelines of their governing country’s regulations of:

Local or international business to business trade.

Business to consumer activity.

In order to ensure that you are not liable for chargebacks, follow these five steps:

Use a name that your customers will recognize

Your name will be on the customer’s credit card statement. Make sure that customers recognise the name that will show up on their bill.

Be available

Make sure that customers can get hold of you- have an email address or phone number on your website that’s easy to find.

Be as detailed as possible

Make sure that you describe what you’re selling accurately. Be as detailed as you can - provide images or screenshot if you can, and provide dimensions of products. Make sure that your customers have as much information as possible, so that they know what it is they’re buying.

Don’t make a promise you can’t keep

Don’t sell unrealistic expectations- these can lead to very unhappy customers and lots of complaints! Make sure that your product does what you say it can do, otherwise you’re going to find yourself in trouble.

Don’t hold onto your customers’ money- and provide refunds

Make sure that your customer gets what they paid for as soon as possible; the longer you have their money without providing their product, the more time they have to issue a chargeback. If a customer is not happy with the product or service you have provided, offer a refund- they will get their money back one way or another and it is best to deal with the issue promptly. Finally, make sure that you have detailed records for each purchase, exchange and return. This allows you to offer the best service possible, whilst also protecting yourself and your business. Although credit card chargebacks can be complicated, detailed records will help you and the credit card company quickly ascertain if charges are genuine or fraudulent.

The Re-presentment Process Explained

The process for merchants to dispute a chargeback is called ‘chargeback re-presentment’ and requires merchants to gather evidence and other necessary information in order to demonstrate that the transaction was properly completed. If you are not actively working to prevent chargebacks (whether they are due to ‘true fraud’, such as stolen credit cards or identity theft, or ‘friendly fraud’ such as buyer’s remorse), you could end up incurring costs to your business. Too many chargebacks could see you completely losing your processing privileges.

Chargeback re-presentment can be a complicated business and requires your in-house team to understand the bank’s requirements when disputing chargebacks. Every time a chargeback notification is issued, it will include a specific reason code to show why the chargeback has been filed. Your team has to be able to understand this code and what you need to do in order to respond to the notification. Your team needs to be accurate, otherwise the bank in question will reject your re-presentment in favour of the customer. The issuing bank may also decide to continue the dispute.

To prevent this from happening, you should monitor your in-house team to ensure that they are working effectively. By using the correct tools, you can track all chargebacks, reason codes, filing deadline and the ratio of wins/losses on your re-presentments.

If your in-house team is losing more than 50% of re-presentment cases, you are losing money and should consider outsourcing your chargeback work to an experienced re-presentment company. An experienced re-presentment company will:

Eliminate paper work by digitising all files

Have a dedicated, expert team tracking all chargebacks.

Manage all of your chargebacks online.

Be able to identify all reasons for chargebacks and understand how they are affecting your business.

Keep track of chargeback rates across all of your merchant accounts.

Provide an experienced team to dispute your chargebacks quickly and efficiently.

Aim to win at least 60% of your chargeback disputes

Be able to track and recover your lost revenue.
